# /strategic-thinking -- First Principles + Pareto Analysis

Strip a business challenge down to fundamental truths, identify the 20% of actions that drive 80% of results, and eliminate everything else.

## Invocation

```
/strategic-thinking We need to grow enterprise revenue 40% but the sales cycle is 9 months
/strategic-thinking Should we build vs. buy our analytics platform?
/strategic-thinking [describe your challenge, current approach, and constraints]
```

## Workflow

### Step 1: Understand the Challenge

Accept the problem in any form — a goal, a decision, a frustration, or a vague sense that something isn't working.

Collect or infer:
- **Challenge/goal**: What specifically needs to be solved or achieved
- **Current approach**: What the user is doing now
- **Resources**: Time, budget, team, tools available
- **Constraints**: Limitations or restrictions
- **Success metrics**: How the user measures success

If the user provides documents or context, extract what's available. Ask up to 2 questions for critical gaps.

### Step 2: Analyze with First Principles + Pareto

Apply the **strategic-thinking** skill:

1. **First Principles Breakdown**: Strip to fundamental truths, expose assumptions, question everything, rebuild from zero
2. **Pareto Analysis**: Identify the vital 20% (high leverage) vs. the trivial 80% (low leverage), rank by impact ÷ effort
3. **Highest-Leverage Actions** (3–5): Specific actions with first principles reasoning, concrete steps, and quantified impact
4. **Elimination Strategy**: What to stop, which assumptions to test, which "best practices" to ignore, what complexity to remove

### Step 3: Deliver the Analysis

```
## Strategic Analysis: [Challenge/Goal]

### First Principles Breakdown
**Fundamental truths**:
1. [Truth] — [Why we know this]

**Assumptions exposed**:
1. [Assumption] — [Why it might be wrong]

**If we started from zero**: [Ideal approach, unconstrained]

---

### Pareto Analysis
**The vital 20%**:
| Action / Input | Estimated Impact | Current Investment |
|---------------|-----------------|-------------------|

**The trivial 80%**:
| Action / Input | Estimated Impact | Current Investment |
|---------------|-----------------|-------------------|

---

### Highest-Leverage Actions
#### 1. [Action]
- **Why**: [First principles reasoning]
- **How**: [Concrete steps]
- **Impact**: [Quantified]
- **Replaces**: [What it displaces]

---

### Elimination Strategy
- **Stop immediately**: [Activity — why it's waste]
- **Assumptions to test**: [Assumption — how to validate]
- **"Best practices" to ignore**: [Practice — why it doesn't apply]
- **Complexity to remove**: [Process — overhead vs. value]

---

### Bottom Line
[The single most important insight and the one action that would change everything]
```

Save as markdown.

### Step 4: Offer Follow-ups

- "Want me to **pressure-test this analysis** — argue the other side?"
- "Should I **turn the top actions into a 90-day execution plan**?"
- "Want me to **run a pre-mortem** on the recommended approach?"
- "Should I **apply this to a specific product or initiative** in more detail?"

## Notes

- Be brutally honest — call out waste even if it's politically uncomfortable
- Specifics over principles: "Cut 5 weekly meetings to 1 and save 8 hours" beats "streamline communication"
- Quantify impact whenever possible — time saved, revenue, cost avoided, risk reduced
- Challenge conventional wisdom — "everyone does it this way" is not a reason
- Every recommendation must have concrete next steps; if you can't explain how, it's not actionable
- Tone is direct, supportive, executive-level — no fluff, bias toward decisive recommendations
