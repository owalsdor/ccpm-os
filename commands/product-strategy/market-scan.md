# /market-scan -- Macro Environment Analysis

Orchestrates four strategic-analysis skills into a single cross-framework scan: SWOT + PESTLE + Porter's Five Forces + Ansoff Matrix.

## Invocation

```
/market-scan EdTech market for corporate learning
/market-scan [upload a market brief or strategy doc]
/market-scan Our fintech product — preparing for board strategy review
```

## Workflow

### Step 1: Understand the Context

Ask: what product / company / market is being analyzed, the purpose (strategic planning, market entry, investor prep, annual review), whether to focus on specific frameworks or run all four, and the user's current position in the market.

### Step 2: Chain the Skills

Apply in sequence, each building on the previous:

1. **swot-analysis** — internal (strengths, weaknesses) + external (opportunities, threats) with actions.
2. **pestle-analysis** — political, economic, social, technological, legal, environmental factors with impact and timeline.
3. **porters-five-forces** — rivalry, supplier power, buyer power, substitutes, new entrants, plus overall industry attractiveness.
4. **ansoff-matrix** — penetration, market development, product development, diversification with risk-adjusted growth plays.

Let each skill produce its own output.

### Step 3: Synthesize Cross-Framework

Add a synthesis section that identifies:
- **Converging signals** — what multiple frameworks agree on
- **Strategic imperatives** — actions that appear critical across analyses
- **Key risks** — threats and forces to mitigate
- **Best opportunities** — the risk-adjusted growth plays to pursue

Close with strategic recommendations backed by evidence from multiple frameworks, and a monitoring plan for key signals.

### Step 4: Offer Next Steps

- "Want me to **build a product strategy** based on these findings?" (chains to strategy)
- "Should I **analyze specific competitors** identified in Porter's?"
- "Want me to **design a pricing strategy** for the market penetration play?" (chains to pricing)

## Notes

- Use web research to ground analysis in current market data, not general knowledge
- PESTLE should cite specific regulations, market data, and trend signals — not generic observations
- Porter's is most useful when you identify *specific* forces, not just rate them abstractly
- Ansoff should include concrete opportunities, not generic "enter new markets"
- The synthesis section is the most valuable part — that's where the frameworks talk to each other
