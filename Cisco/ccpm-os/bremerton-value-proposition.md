# Value Proposition: Bremerton (UCS X-Series Chassis with New Power Supplies)

## For IT Infrastructure Managers:

### 1. Who
IT infrastructure managers responsible for data center compute strategy, capacity planning, and uptime SLAs — typically in enterprise environments running Cisco UCS X-Series blade deployments at scale.

### 2. Why
They need to run fully populated X-Series blade configurations in grid-mode power redundancy to maximize both compute density and data center availability. The job: **get the most compute out of every chassis without compromising power resilience.**

### 3. What Before
Today, customers running the current X-Series chassis in grid mode face a painful trade-off:

- **Option A — Grid mode with current chassis:** Reduced power availability forces customers to leave blade slots empty or use lower-power configurations, wasting chassis capacity.
- **Option B — Switch to N+1 PSU redundancy:** Restores full blade population but downgrades from grid-mode resilience, lowering overall data center availability and increasing risk of service disruption during PSU failure.

Neither option delivers the full promise of X-Series density + resilience together.

### 4. How
Bremerton is a new X-Series chassis with redesigned, higher-capacity power supplies that deliver enough power to run **fully populated blades in grid mode** — eliminating the density-vs-availability trade-off entirely.

Key capabilities:
- **New PSU design** engineered for full blade population under grid-mode power redundancy
- **Drop-in upgrade path** for existing X-Series environments (Cisco-to-Cisco)
- **Supports latest-generation Intel and AMD CPUs** — maintaining X-Series' generational lead over competitive blade architectures

### 5. What After
With Bremerton, IT infrastructure managers can:
- **Fully populate every blade slot** while maintaining grid-mode power redundancy — no more choosing between density and availability
- **Maximize return on chassis investment** by using 100% of available compute capacity
- **Maintain the highest data center availability tier** without architectural compromises
- **Simplify capacity planning** — one chassis config that delivers both density and resilience

### 6. Alternatives
- **Current X-Series chassis (grid mode):** Works, but forces reduced blade population — leaving compute capacity on the table.
- **Current X-Series chassis (N+1 mode):** Full population, but at the cost of lower power redundancy and reduced data center availability.
- **HPE Synergy / Dell MX7000:** Cannot match X-Series' latest-generation Intel and AMD CPU support in their blade architectures — Bremerton extends this generational lead further.
